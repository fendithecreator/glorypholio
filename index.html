Keith Farrelle Cozart

Keith Farrelle Cozart (born August 15, 1995),better known by his stage name Chief Keef, is an American rapper and record producer. Born and raised in Chicago's South Side, he began his recording career as a teenager and initially garnered regional attention and praise for his mixtapes in the early 2010s. Cozart is often credited with popularizing the hip hop subgenre drill for mainstream audiences, and is considered a progenitor of the genre. Stereogum has referred to Cozart as a "modern rap folk hero".

"Finally Rich": Chief Keef's explosive debut album that catapulted him into the forefront of hip-hop, showcasing his signature blend of menacing beats and unapologetic lyrics, cementing his status as a rap phenomenon.

"Back from the Dead": A resurrection of raw talent and relentless energy, Chief Keef's mixtape marked his triumphant return, unleashing a hauntingly hypnotic sound that revitalized the rap scene and solidified his influence.

"Young and Flexin'": Chief Keef's early mixtape exudes youthful swagger and unbridled ambition, delivering a relentless barrage of street anthems and braggadocious bars that announce his arrival as a force to be reckoned with in the rap game.
